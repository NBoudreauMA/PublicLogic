A personal note from Nate

Why I started this work — and who it’s for.

My name is Nathan Boudreau. I built Public_Logic — Verified Automation & Unified Logic for Towns the only way I know how: from inside town government, where the meetings run long, the emails don’t stop, and people still expect the lights to come on and the roads to get plowed.

There were hard seasons. An ethics uncertainty knocked me off course. A cancer scare slowed me down. I know what burnout feels like before you even put the key in the office door. I also know what it means to show up anyway — to pull a packet together at midnight, fix a turnover by hand, or help a resident who needs an answer today.

Public service is people doing difficult work for their neighbors. The tools should help — not add weight.

I kept seeing the same thing: too many systems, too many hands, and no shared way to move the work. So I started shaping a simple idea — put the work on rails. One path that carries requests, files, and decisions from Town Hall, through compliance, to a clear public result.

Public_Logic treats this as digital infrastructure, not another app to learn. The underscore in our name is a reminder: one line that connects everything.

VAULT is built on an agnostic data framework — a flexible system that layers rules, structure, and compliance to serve municipal government.

This isn’t about shiny software. It’s about fewer surprises, strong records, and services people can actually use. If we can’t explain it, log it, and share it, it doesn’t ship.

— Nathan R. Boudreau, MPA, MCPPO Town Administrator & Founder, Public_Logic

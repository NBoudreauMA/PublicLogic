A personal note from Nate

Why Public_Logic exists — and who it’s for.

My name is Nathan Boudreau. I built Public_Logic — Verified Automation & Unified Logic for Towns the only way I know how — from inside town government, under real pressure, with accountability on the line. An ethics uncertainty knocked me sideways. A cancer scare forced me to slow down. I’ve carried the kind of burnout that finds you in the parking lot before you even make it to your desk.

Government staff deserve tools that work — rails that move the work without adding more to carry.

I’ve managed towns from four to forty thousand. I’ve sat at every table: Select Board packets at 1 a.m., last-minute warrant edits, budget pivots, licensing holds, turnovers, and public records that can’t get lost. The pattern was always the same — too many systems, too many hands, and no shared logic to hold it together.

Public_Logic turns municipal practice into digital infrastructure instead of a pile of apps. The underscore in our name is intentional — a single rail that connects Town Hall → Compliance (Vault) → Public Output. Requests, approvals, files, and records move the same way every time: transparent, auditable, and explainable.

VAULT is built on an agnostic data framework — a flexible system that layers rules, structure, and compliance to serve municipal government.

We’re practitioner-led and compliance-first. The goal isn’t shiny software; it’s fewer surprises, stronger records, and services residents can actually use. If it can’t be explained, logged, and shared, it doesn’t ship.

— Nathan R. Boudreau, MPA, MCPPO Town Administrator & Founder, Public_Logic

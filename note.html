Founder’s Note

My name is Nathan Boudreau, and I built Public_Logic the only way I know how — from inside town government, under real pressure, with accountability on the line.

An ethics uncertainty derailed my career. A cancer scare forced me to stop pretending I could carry everything forever. I’ve lived through the kind of burnout that takes your breath away in the parking lot before you even walk into the building.

But I also know what it means to show up the next day anyway. To patch together a budget at midnight. To fix a turnover by hand because the system didn’t. To smile through a meeting while your whole life feels like it’s unraveling.

That’s why I created Public_Logic. Because government staff deserve tools that work. Because compliance shouldn’t be an afterthought. Because the people in municipal buildings — the clerks, the treasurers, the administrators, the DPW crews — deserve infrastructure that makes their work sustainable, not harder.

Public_Logic isn’t about shiny tech. It’s about building rails so the work can move: Town Hall → Compliance → Public Output. It’s about making sure nothing slips, nothing gets lost, and no one is left carrying the weight alone.

— Nathan R. Boudreau, MPA, MCPPO
Town Administrator & Founder, Public_Logic
